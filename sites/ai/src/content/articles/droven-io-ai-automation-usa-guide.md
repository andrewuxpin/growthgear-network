### Step 3: Calculate ROI Before Selecting a Tool

Knowledge platforms like Droven.io help you understand automation categories. But a defensible investment case requires a quantified ROI calculation — a step most teams either skip or complete after they've already committed to a vendor.

**Annual automation ROI = (Hours saved per year × fully-loaded hourly rate) − (Tool cost + implementation cost)**

Worked example using Tier 1 workflow automation:
- **Hours saved**: 15 hours/week across the operations team
- **Fully-loaded hourly rate**: $55/hour (including benefits and overhead)
- **Annual time value**: 15 × 52 × $55 = **$42,900**
- **Tool cost**: $600/year (Make.com Pro plan)
- **Setup cost**: $3,000 (one-time external consultant or internal developer time)
- **Year 1 ROI**: $42,900 − $3,600 = **$39,300 — a 1,092% first-year return**

Droven.io's industry content and automation tier data give you the numerator — hours saved by process type based on validated benchmarks. Your finance team supplies the denominator. Building this calculation before any vendor conversation transforms the automation discussion from "should we do this?" to "which tool gives us the fastest payback on a decision we've already made."
